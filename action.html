1. The Dark Knight (2008)

The Dark Knight is a 2008 superhero film directed by Christopher Nolan and starring Christian Bale as the titular character. It was released on July 15, 2008 in the United States and was widely acclaimed for its dark and realistic portrayal of Batman.

Directed by Christopher Nolan.
Starring Heath Ledger, Aaron Eckhart, and Michael Caine.

Runtime: 2 hours 32 minutes

Rating: 9.0/10

2. The Lord of the Rings: The Return of the King (2003)

This epic conclusion to Peter Jackson’s trilogy follows the hobbits as they face Sauron’s armies and prepare to destroy the One Ring. With performances from Viggo Mortensen, Ian McKellen, and Orlando Bloom, this movie will keep you up until the very end.

Directed by Peter Jackson.
Starring Elijah Wood, Viggo Mortensen, Kirsten Dunst and Sean Astin.

Runtime: 3 hours 26 minutes

Rating: 9.0/10

3. Inception (2010)

Inception is a 2010 science fiction action film written and directed by Christopher Nolan. It stars Leonardo DiCaprio as Cobb, a thief who is offered a chance to redeem himself by planting an idea into someone's mind instead of stealing it. The film explores themes of perception and reality through multiple layers of dream sequences within dreams.

Starring Leonardo DiCaprio, Joseph Gordon-Levitt, Tom Hardy, Marion Cotillard, Ken Watanabe and Cillian Mur dally.

Runtime: 2 hours 28 minutes

Rating: 8.8/10

4. The Lord of the Rings: The Fellowship of the Ring (2001)

The first installment in Peter Jackson's three-part adaptation of J. R. R. Tolkien's classic fantasy novel features a group of hob bits led by Frodo Baggins as they attempt to destroy the powerful One Ring created by the Dark Lord Sauron. Alongside Frodo are Samwise Gamgee, Meriadoc Brandybuck, Peregrin To okien and Arwen, and Gandalf the Grey, a powerful wizard.

Directed by Peter Jackson. Starring Elijah Wood, Ian McKellen, Viggo Mortensen, Kilian McCann, Sean Bean, Michael Gwynn, Mark Andrews, Timothy Olyphant, Billy Boyd and Olivia Adams-Dailey.

Runtime: 2 hours 75 minutes

Rating: 8.8/10

5. The Lord of the Rings: The Two Towers (2002)

This movie follows on from 'The Lord of the Rings: The Fellowship of the Ring', where our heroes continue their quest against the forces of darkness. They must face orcs, goblins and other creatures while also dealing with internal conflicts among themselves. The special effects are impressive but not overwhelming, and the story keeps you engaged right up until the end. However, some viewers may find the pacing slow at times. Overall, this is an excellent continuation of the trilogy that will keep you on the edge of your seat.

Directed by Peter Jackson. Stars Elijah Wood, Ian McKellen, Viggo Mortensen, Sean Astin, Nicholas Hammond, Richard Harris, Charles Dance, Frank Harper and CateTBush.

Runtime: 2 hours 59 minutes

Rating: 8.8/10

6. Spider-Man: Across the Spider-Verse (2023)

Miles Morales returns for the next chapter of the Oscar®-winning Spider-Verse saga, Spider-Man™: Across the Spider-Verse (2018, Best Animated Film, Spider-Man™: Into The Spider-Verse). After reuniting with Gwen Stacy, Brooklyn’s full-time, friendly neighborhood Spider-Man is catapulted across the Multiverse, where he encounters the Spider Society, a team of Spider-People charged with protecting the Multiverse’s very existence. But when the heroes clash on how to handle a new threat, Miles finds himself pitted against the other Spiders and must set out on his own to save those he loves most. Anyone can wear the mask – it’s how you wear it that makes you a hero.

Directed by Joaquim Dos Santos, Justin K. Thompson, Kemp Powers

Runtime: 2 hours 20 minutes

Rating: 8.7/10

7. Star Wars: Episode V - The Empire Strikes Back (1980)

After the destruction of the Death Star, Imperial forces continue to pursue the Rebels. After the Rebellion’s defeat on the ice planet Hoth, Luke journeys to the planet Dagobah to train with Jedi Master Yoda, who has lived in hiding since the fall of the Republic. In an attempt to convert Luke to the dark side, Darth Vader lures young Skywalker into a trap in the Cloud City of Bespin.

Directed by Irvin Kershner.

Runtime: 2 hours 4 minutes

Rating: 8.7/10

8. Terminator 2: Judgment Day (1991)

The T-800 Terminator, sent back from the future to protect Sarah Connor and her son John from a cyborg assassin sent to kill them, must then protect a human boy named John Connor from Skynet, which has advanced to include machines like himself, and is now able to create more Terminators.

Directed by James Cameron.

Runtime: 2 hours 17 minutes

Rating: 8.6/10

10. Avengers: Endgame (2019)

After the devastating events of Avengers: Infinity War (2018), the universe is in ruins. With the help of remaining allies, the Avengers assemble once more in order to reverse Thanos' actions and restore balance to the universe

Directed by Joe Russo, Anthony Russo

Runtime: 3 hours 02 minutes

Teen Miles Morales becomes the Spider-Man of his universe and must join with five spider-powered individuals from other dimensions to stop a threat for all realities.
